# Complete case set

This document contains two complete mck-consult skill application cases.

## Case 1: Analysis of China’s Online Audio Program Market

**Business Question**: "Company S acquires the Podcast platform and wants to understand the reasons for the growth of China's audio program market"

### Issue Tree (simplified)
```
Lv1: 1. Where is growth reflected?
  Lv2: 1.1 Market segment
    Lv3: 1.1.1 What are the subdivisions?
    Lv3: 1.1.2 Historical trends in various fields
  Lv2: 1.2 B-side enterprise
  Lv2: 1.3 C-side users

Lv1: 2. Why growth
  Lv2: 2.1 Demand-side driver
  Lv2: 2.2 supply side mode
```

### Hypotheses
- H1: Audiobooks and online courses drive growth, while podcasts account for a low proportion
- H2: Fragmented time + anxiety drives the need for self-improvement
- H3: Head podcaster demonstration + course high customer unit price model

### Dummy Pages (excerpt)
**Page 3**: Audiobooks and online courses drive growth, podcasts account for only 5%
- Layout: Timeline + Stacked Column Chart
- Data: Revenue by segment from 2019-2024

**Page 5**: Performance-style audiobooks drive content explosion through demonstrations by leading broadcasters
- Layout: left and right columns
- Left side: content quantity + broadcaster income double Y-axis
- Right: Bauhinia case

**Page 8**: The single user value of online courses is 3 times that of audio books
- Layout: Comparison table
- Data: User scale/payment rate/customer unit price/single user value

### Final output
18 pages of McKinsey style PPT, clearly answering the reasons for growth

## Case 2: Analysis of China Charging Pile Market Entry Opportunities

**Business Question**: "We want to enter the Chinese charging pile market and analyze growth/players/opportunities"

### Issue Tree
```
Lv1: 1. Market growth
  Lv2: 1.1 Scale and Growth
  Lv2: 1.2 market segment (public/private, fast charging/slow charging)

Lv1: 2. Main players and modes
  Lv2: 2.1 Player pattern (Top5, state-owned enterprises vs private enterprises)
  Lv2: 2.2 Success Model (Business Model/Layout/Technology)

Lv1: 3. Opportunity to enter
  Lv2: 3.1 Market blank
  Lv2: 3.2 Experience pain points
```

### Hypotheses
- H1: In the period of rapid growth, public fast charging is the core
- H2: The head dominates but the pattern is not solidified
- H3: Differentiated positioning and breakthrough in segmented scenarios

### Dummy Pages (excerpt)
**Page 3**: Charging piles are growing rapidly, and public fast charging is the core driver
- Layout: Timeline + Stacked Column Chart
- Note: Public fast charging increased five times in four years

**Page 6**: TELD/Star Charge/Cloud Fast Charging account for half of the market
- Layout: Comparison table
- Top5 companies/shares/layout scenarios

**Page 9**: Opportunities in the "Logistics Park + Scenic Area" vertical scenario
- Layout: 2×2 matrix
- Competition intensity on the X-axis, profit potential on the Y-axis

### Final output
16 pages of McKinsey style PPT + 3 action suggestions

---

## Key learning points

1. **Issue Tree requires MECE** - each layer should avoid overlaps and gaps
2. **Hypotheses must be verifiable** - Know what data can prove them
3. **Dummy Pages should be detailed** - Specify chart types and data requirements
4. **Data must come from multiple sources** - Use 2-3 sources for verification
5. **Iteration is perfect** - The first version does not seek perfection, 2-3 rounds of optimization
